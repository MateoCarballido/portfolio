# Portfolio Audit Brief For Claude Code

This file distills the audit in `portfolio_audit_mateo.md` into concrete implementation work.

## Goal

Rework the portfolio messaging so the value proposition is immediately clear:

- Mateo builds a demo before charging.
- The service feels low-risk, credible, and conversion-focused.
- The copy speaks from the client's perspective, not Mateo's.

## Core Problems To Fix

1. The copy is too centered on what Mateo does instead of what the client gets.
2. The main objection is not answered: "How can this be relevant to my business if we never talked?"
3. There are competing CTAs, which creates friction.
4. The site lacks personal trust signals like a short bio and photo.
5. Using a Gmail address weakens the professional positioning.
6. The audience is too generic; the copy should name local business categories more clearly.

## Highest-Priority Changes

### P1. Replace hero headline and subheadline

Recommended direction:

- Headline: `Ya construí la demo de tu web. Ahora te la muestro.`
- Subheadline: `Sitios web para restaurantes, estudios, clínicas y negocios locales en Buenos Aires. La demo es gratis. El pago, solo si te convence.`

What matters:

- Make the "see it before you pay" model explicit.
- Make the audience more specific.
- Lead with outcome and low risk.

### P2. Replace Gmail with a domain email

Suggested direction:

- Replace `teocarballido@gmail.com` with a branded email like `hola@mateocarballido.com`.

What matters:

- This is a credibility fix, not just a cosmetic one.

### P3. Rewrite the process section from the client's point of view

Recommended structure:

1. `Encontrás la demo esperándote`
2. `Explorás con libertad`
3. `Ajustamos y publicamos`

What matters:

- Describe the user's experience.
- Avoid copy framed as "I do this" or "we do that."
- End on the positive resolution of going live.

### P4. Use one main CTA

Recommended CTA:

- `Ver mi demo gratis`

Recommended behavior:

- Main CTA should route to WhatsApp.
- Secondary contact options should be visually de-emphasized.

What matters:

- Remove choice paralysis.
- Make "gratis" and "sin compromiso" obvious.

### P5. Add trust-building identity

Add a short personal intro near the hero or in an about section:

`Soy Mateo, diseñador web de Buenos Aires. Construyo sitios rápidos, claros y orientados a traerte más consultas. Trabajo con negocios locales que quieren presencia online sin vueltas ni tiempos eternos.`

What matters:

- Make the service feel personal and real.
- Consider adding a photo.

### P6. Add an objection-handling section

Suggested section:

- Title: `¿Y si la demo no se parece a lo que imaginabas?`
- Body: Explain that the demo is the base and the final version is customized with real brand assets, copy, services, and identity.

What matters:

- This should appear before the final CTA or near the process section.

## Messaging Principles

- Write in second person whenever possible.
- Emphasize outcomes over process.
- Prefer concrete benefits over designer language.
- Focus on reduced risk, faster launch, and more inquiries.
- Keep the tone direct, professional, and accessible.

## Suggested Implementation Order

1. Update hero copy.
2. Unify CTA strategy.
3. Rewrite process cards/steps.
4. Add objection-handling section.
5. Add mini-bio and photo.
6. Update contact email.
